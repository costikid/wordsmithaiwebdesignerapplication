# Wordsmith — Integrations Page Redesign

A reimagining of the Wordsmith integrations page, built as an application piece for the **Designer, Web & Brand** role.

This isn't a brief I was given. It's one I picked up.

I looked at the current integrations page and saw a page that undersold the product. So I redesigned it.

## What I changed and why

### The hero: from list to landing

A staggered, slightly rotated cascade of floating integration pills — Notion, Slack, SharePoint, Outlook, Juro, Teams, and the rest — each one a clickable link to the real product. On hover, each pill lifts and scales with a soft cubic-bezier ease.

### The browse section: from grid to experience

Scrolling down, the page transitions from persuasion to utility. The "Find your tools" section gives the visitor something the original didn't: **a way to actually find what they need.**

- **Live search** filters integration cards as you type. No submit button, no page reload. Just instant feedback.
- **Category filter chips** — All, Communication, Productivity, Documents, Automation, Legal — let you narrow by workflow, not by alphabet. The active chip inverts to ink-black, a single-state decision that reads clearly without colour noise.
- **Graceful empty states.** When search returns nothing, the page doesn't just say "no results." It offers a path forward: a "Request an integration" button that expands into a lightweight form (tool name, email, optional use case). A visitor who came looking for Monday.com leaves as a lead instead of a bounce.
